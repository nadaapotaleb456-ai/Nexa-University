# Academic Curriculum Overview  
## Nexa University – Electrical & Computer Engineering (ECE)

The Nexa University curriculum is publicly visible to ensure academic transparency.
This document presents **course titles and structural organization only**.

All instructional materials, lectures, assignments, examinations, and project
specifications are restricted to admitted students and are not publicly accessible.

---

## Program Structure

- Degree Duration: 5 Academic Years
- Semesters: 10 (Fall & Spring)
- Courses per Semester: 7
- Emphasis: Mathematical rigor, systems architecture, processor design, and research readiness

---

## Year 1 — Foundations of Engineering and Computation

### Semester 1
1. Calculus I (Single-Variable Analysis)
2. Linear Algebra I
3. Classical Mechanics
4. Introduction to Programming
5. Engineering Thinking and Problem Solving
6. Digital Logic Fundamentals
7. Academic Writing and Research Basics

**Assessment:**  
- Midterm Examination (Week 7)  
- Final Examination (End of Semester)  
- Course Project (Design-Oriented)

---

### Semester 2

2. Linear Algebra II
3. Electricity and Magnetism I

- Midterm Examination  
- Final Examination

## Assessment Policy (Summary)

- Each course includes a mandatory applied project.
- Each semester concludes with a comprehensive final examination.
- The program culminates in a research-based capstone thesis.

Detailed syllabi and instructional content are accessible only to admitted students.
